What is Acne and Acne Scars

Acne is a common long-term inflammatory skin condition, and it is suffered by millions globally. It often shows up on the face, neck, chest, and back and presents as:


Whiteheads and blackheads

Tiny, hard bumps (nodules) and painful large lumps (cysts) under your skin

If not treated or wrongly treated, it can cause permanent scars like:


Atrophic scars: A holes or deep indentation in the skin and occur when enough acne causes enough tissue damage.

Hypertrophic scars: Raised, shiny scars that are thicker than normal

Ice-pick scars: Small, deep holes in the surface of the skinAcne may cause permanent scars if left untreated or not well managed, such as:

Rolling scars: Broad, undulating depressions.


Causes and Risk Factors of Acne

Etiology and Risk Factors for Acne

Hyperseborrhea Overabundance of sebum (oil) in the skin

Hormonal changes during adolescence, menstruation, pregnancy

Bacterial colonization (Propionibacterium acnes)

Stress and poor sleep

Comedogenic skin care or makeup products applied on the skin

Bad diet, particularly if it was high in dairy and sugar

Genetic predisposition

Some medications (steroids, lithium)
