Common Hair Fall &Hair Loss Causes

Hair loss is a common problem for both men and women of any age. We're supposed to lose some hair and shed some hair, but constant or heavy hair loss could be a sign of something else going on. Common types include


Androgenetic Alopecia (Male/Female Pattern Baldness): The most common type of hereditary hair loss, in which hair falls out and is not replaced, leading to a gradual thinning of hair.

Telogen Effluvium: Shedding, temporary, with stress, sickness, surgery, or hormonal change.

Alopecia Areata: Spontaneous bald spots resulting from an autoimmune disease.

Scarring Alopecia: Hair loss that is permanent as a result of Scarring alopecia is most commonly caused by irradiation of the scalp area effects. Scarring alopecia is a condition resulting from‚Äîcaused most frequently by‚Äîirradiation of the scalp region.

Traction Alopecia: Hair loss caused by excessive pulling or tight hairstyles.

Diffuse Hair Loss: Overall thinning is often associated with underlying medical or nutritional factors.
